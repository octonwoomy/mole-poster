What the HECK is a Mole!!?

Definition

A mole is a unit of measurement used in chemistry to express amounts of a chemical substance. One mole of any substance contains Avogadro's number of entities, which is approximately 6.02 × 10^23.

Definition of Avogadro's Number

Avogadro's number, denoted as NA, is the number of atoms, ions, or molecules in one mole of a substance. It is approximately 6.02 × 10^23.

Interesting Fact about the Mole (Number)

One interesting fact about Avogadro's number is that it is independent of the substance being measured. Whether you're talking about atoms, molecules, ions, or even something else entirely, one mole of that substance will always contain the same number of entities, which is Avogadro's number.
